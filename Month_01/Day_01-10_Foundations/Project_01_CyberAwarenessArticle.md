"#Project 01: Cyber Awareness Guide"

For the past few day i have been reviewing some foundational cybersecurity concepts, and this article is meant to explain some this issue in the most simple form, and also understandable to someone who has litle or no of what cybersecurity entails.

To begin with, cybersecurity is all about protecting our resources (Computers, data, network and systems) from cyber "criminals", as well as from unforeseen disaters , be it natural or man-made, intentionally or by accident. In essence, it about keeping organizations (and their stake holders) safe in the cyber space, and to avoid losses (such as financial or reputaional loss)

Some of the principles that cybersecurity is built aroud include: Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability. These three make up what is called the CIA triad. When ever you are considering a security solution, it is worth checking these three boxes. There is definitely more, but the CIA triad is a good starting point. Confidentiality ensures that data is protected from unauthorized access, ,meaning only authorized actors can have acceess to it. Integrity ensures and verifies the authenticity of the data, making sure it not changed or tampered with, be it in transit, in use, or at rest. Availability ensures that data and resources are available for use when they are needed.

Talking about confidentiality, this can be enforced using access control models. These are enforcing policies that allows or disallows access for diferent actors, based on predefined criteria. It basically checks if you are supposed enter a particular system, if not you are not allowed. There different popular methods of doing this. Two major models include Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), where access is granted based on your role or occupation in the organization, and Discretionary Access Control (DAC), where the owner or creator of the data decides who has access to it. Other notable models are Mandatory Access Control and Rule-Based Access Control. An underlying concept under Access Control is the principle of least privilege (PoLP), this priciple holds that people should be given the minimum access or privileges required to perform their duty. Defense In depth is another principle which holds that security goes beyond just one layer. We need different layers of security, such that if one fails, the next can protect the system or limit the damage.

In Cybersecurity, we equality have frameworks and compliances policies. These are like security blueprints or standards put forth by some renoun tech/securtity bodies, for organizations to use when developing their security solutions. Some notable frameworks are the NIST,ISO, COBIS, and CIS Controls.
